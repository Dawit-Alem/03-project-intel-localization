Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1971

First Microprocessor

The 8086 processor establishes the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers worldwide.

1978

8086 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 with 32-bit architecture, powering stronger performance and multitasking.

1985

386 Processor

Intel hits its highest greenhouse gas emissions, then invests in renewable energy and efficiency to reverse the trend.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals to accelerate climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across operations by 2040, building on years of sustainability progress.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity use worldwide, cutting carbon emissions and advancing energy sustainability.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit to align suppliers, governments, and leaders on next-gen sustainable manufacturing.

2024
