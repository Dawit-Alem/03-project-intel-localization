1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation in 1968, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation. The name "Intel" stands for Integrated Electronics, reflecting the company's mission to push the boundaries of what's possible in computing.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution. Designed by Federico Faggin, Ted Hoff, and Stanley Mazor, the 4004 packed 2,300 transistors into a chip the size of a fingernail, delivering as much computing power as the first electronic computer, ENIAC.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 processor establishes the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers worldwide. This 16-bit microprocessor became the foundation for the personal computer revolution and its architecture is still at the core of modern processors today, making it one of the most influential chip designs in history.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 with 32-bit architecture, powering stronger performance and multitasking. The Intel 386 processor contained 275,000 transistors — more than 100 times the number in the original 4004 — and enabled a new generation of software that transformed personal computing and business productivity.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel hits its highest greenhouse gas emissions in 2006, then makes a decisive commitment to reverse the trend. The company invests heavily in renewable energy, chemical alternatives, and energy efficiency programs across its global manufacturing operations, setting the stage for decades of environmental progress.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals to accelerate climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction. RISE represents Intel's most comprehensive sustainability framework, aligning business objectives with global environmental and social responsibility targets.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across its global operations by 2040. This pledge covers Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions and builds on Intel's decades of environmental initiatives, renewable energy investments, and partnerships across the semiconductor industry to drive systemic change.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity use worldwide, significantly cutting carbon emissions and advancing energy sustainability. This milestone is achieved through a combination of direct renewable energy purchases, power purchase agreements, and on-site solar installations across Intel's global campuses and manufacturing facilities.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together suppliers, governments, and industry leaders to align on next-generation sustainable manufacturing practices. The summit establishes new benchmarks for supply chain transparency, emissions reporting, and collaborative environmental action across the global semiconductor ecosystem.
